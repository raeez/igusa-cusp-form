## Risks if retained

1. False dependency signal.  By sitting as Appendix A, the section looks like
   part of the proof infrastructure.  It is not.

2. Referee attack surface.  A reader can spend time checking Saito-Kurokawa
   lift normalizations, spinor L-function shifts, gamma factors, and period
   conventions instead of the Borcherds determinant and Dirac/Pfaffian
   realization problem.

3. Convention fragility.  The formula is sensitive to whether `L_spin` is
   Andrianov's classical uncompleted L-function, a completed spin L-function,
   or a representation-theoretic normalization.  A harmless shift mismatch
   would look like a theorem-level error.

4. Source gap.  The proof invokes "Deligne's Ramanujan-Petersson theorem" at
   `main.tex:14625` without a visible RP source in the current spin
   bibliography.  The nearby `Del79` entry supports periods, not obviously the
   bound used for nonvanishing.

5. Period-sign trap.  The `Omega^+`/`Omega^-` parity convention at
   `main.tex:14635-14642` is source-convention dependent.  If the sign is
   reversed relative to a chosen Manin/Kohnen-Zagier convention, the main paper
   would inherit an irrelevant error.

6. Square-root confusion.  The main paper argues that the OP/DT scalar square
   does not construct the first-order Dirac square root.  A long scalar-square
   spin L-factor appendix blurs that message.

7. No compensating theorem.  The appendix does not strengthen the determinant,
   denominator, orientation, compact-source, normal-ordering, primitive
   recognition, or row-certificate results.

8. TOC pollution.  It appears before the eight-row appendix.  That makes a
   side arithmetic normalization more visually prominent than actual boundary
   compatibility material.
