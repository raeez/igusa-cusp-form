## Valid Attacks

### A61-01: `A_R` is underdefined for the new target tower

Severity: 2.

Target: `main.tex:5889-5892`, `main.tex:5978-5979`.

Claim under attack: a finite cusp-positive, downward-saturated active window
`A_R\subset\Gamma_{\mathrm{gram}}` is enough to define the target reference
tower and its exhausting limit.

Broken step: earlier manuscript machinery defines target windows more
carefully via `\Gamma_{\Delta,R}`, `\Gamma_R^{test,+}`, and
`A_R={\gamma\mid a_\Delta(\gamma)\ne0}` at `main.tex:10442-10503`, with
cofinality and source-coverage residuals.  The new section reuses `A_R`
without importing that schedule or saying `A_R\subset\Gamma_{\mathrm{act}}`
and `0\notin A_R`.  If zero or non-positive/cancellation degrees enter, the
formal product and PBW span can become degenerate or infinite.

Minimal heal: replace the first sentence with a dependency on the existing
finite target-window schedule, e.g. `Fix one of the finite cusp-positive
target windows A_R\subset\Gamma_{\mathrm{act}}\subset\Gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}`
from the finite-window certificate section, with `0\notin A_R`, and let
`R` run through a cofinal directed system exhausting `\Gamma_{\mathrm{act}}`.

### A61-02: PBW "total Gram degree in `A_R`" is not a finite-window basis

Severity: 2.

Target: `main.tex:5911-5923`.

Claim under attack: the span of PBW monomials whose total Gram degree lies in
`A_R` is the finite PBW target window.

Broken step: total degree alone does not bound PBW word length if opposite
roots, Cartan degree, or cancellation channels are present.  Even with a finite
output degree set, PBW monomials can be infinite unless the window is restricted
to a positive nilpotent half, generated by finite letters with bounded height,
or explicitly quotiented/truncated by length.  Agent50 already permits PBW only
as imported target PBW, not as source PBW.

Minimal heal: define the PBW window as a finite positive-half PBW span with
letter degrees in a finite set and output degree in the successor window, or
state that it is a formal target filtration piece rather than a finite
dimensional Hall state object.  Keep the current line `not necessarily an
endomorphism of one fixed window`; add that fixed windows are not Hopf algebras.

### A61-03: finite-window radical wording can be read as a source/radical theorem

Severity: 3.

Target: `main.tex:5908-5910`, `main.tex:6013-6016`.

Claim under attack: the finite truncation carries "standard invariant form,
standard radical quotient" and the realization functor preserves radical
kernels.

Broken step: the global `\mathfrak g_{\Delta_5}` already has the imported
GN/Kac radical quotient.  A finite direct sum of root spaces is not, by itself,
a bracket-closed Lie superalgebra with its own computed radical quotient; the
restriction of the invariant form may also pair degrees outside the selected
window unless the window is sign-symmetric and pairing-closed.  For compact
geometry, radical kernels remain source data.

Minimal heal: say "the globally imported GN/Kac invariant form and already
quotiented target algebra, restricted to the selected degrees" and reserve
"radical kernels" for the source realization conditions.

### A61-04: `V_\gamma` is only signed-superdimension data unless tied to the target root space

Severity: 3.

Target: `main.tex:5925-5948`.

Claim under attack: choosing any finite super vector space with
`\sdim V_\gamma=f(nm,l)` gives the target-compatible first-order block.

Broken step: signed superdimension determines the scalar product exponent
only.  It does not determine the target parity split, invariant form, PBW
basis, or radical compatibility.  The latest adjudication accepts a formal
Dirac block, but the block must stay scalar/formal unless
`V_\gamma` is chosen as the target root super-space
`\mathfrak g_{\Delta_5,\alpha(\gamma)}` or a declared minimal parity-lift
model.

Minimal heal: either set `V_\gamma:=\mathfrak g_{\Delta_5,\alpha(\gamma)}` in
the target tower, or say explicitly that this block is a signed-dimension
formal product model and carries no canonical parity lift beyond the chosen
convention.

### A61-05: `\lim_R` needs a formal-product topology and directed indexing

Severity: 3.

Target: `main.tex:5969-5994`.

Claim under attack: `\lim_R\operatorname{sPf}^{alg}_R(D)=...` is well-defined
whenever windows exhaust active support.

Broken step: `R` is already overloaded by HN height, target height, finite
windows, and realization index.  The new proposition does not define the
directed set, transition maps for products, or completion in which the product
converges.  The manuscript elsewhere uses cofinal target-window schedules and
formal power-series chambers.  The new proposition should cite that machinery
or restate it.

Minimal heal: replace `\lim_R` by "the coefficientwise limit in the Borcherds
cusp formal product topology over a cofinal increasing family
`A_R\nearrow\Gamma_{\mathrm{act}}`."  If products are over
`\Gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}`, state that inactive factors have exponent zero.
